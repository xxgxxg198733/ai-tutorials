## The Intelligent Evolution of Virtual Meetings

Video conferencing has become the backbone of modern work communication, and in 2026, artificial intelligence has fundamentally transformed what these platforms can do. The basic video call that defined the remote work revolution of the early 2020s has evolved into a rich, intelligent collaboration experience where AI handles everything from audio enhancement and background optimization to real-time transcription, language translation, meeting summarization, and action item tracking. The market leaders have invested billions in AI capabilities, while innovative challengers have built entirely new platforms around AI-native experiences. For businesses of all sizes, choosing the right AI video conferencing platform has become a strategic decision that affects meeting effectiveness, team collaboration, and even competitive advantage. Organizations using advanced AI meeting capabilities report saving an average of 4-6 hours per person per week through reduced meeting time, better meeting outcomes, and eliminated manual note-taking and follow-up tasks. This comparison evaluates the ten leading AI video conferencing platforms across critical dimensions including video and audio AI quality, meeting intelligence features, collaboration tools, integration ecosystems, security and compliance, pricing, and suitability for different team sizes and use cases. Whether you run a distributed startup, manage a hybrid enterprise team, or host large-scale virtual events, this guide will help you identify the platform that delivers the best AI-enhanced meeting experience for your needs.

## Enterprise Leaders: Microsoft Teams Premium and Zoom AI Companion
Let me explain.

In the enterprise video conferencing space, Microsoft Teams Premium and Zoom AI Companion represent the most advanced AI implementations in 2026. Microsoft Teams has become the most deeply AI-integrated collaboration platform available, leveraging the full power of Microsoft Copilot across the meeting lifecycle. Before meetings, Teams AI can analyze calendar data and past communications to suggest attendees, prepare briefing documents, and recommend agenda items. During meetings, AI-powered features include intelligent noise suppression that can distinguish between speech and ambient sounds with remarkable accuracy, real-time transcription with speaker identification in over thirty languages, and live captioning with translation. The AI-facilitated meeting experience includes automatic spotlighting of active speakers, intelligent content sharing that suggests relevant documents based on conversation topics, and AI-generated meeting notes that capture key decisions and action items in real time. After meetings, Copilot provides an interactive AI assistant that can answer questions about what was discussed, generate summary emails, create task lists, and even draft follow-up communications based on meeting outcomes. Teams Premium includes advanced security features like AI-powered phishing detection in meeting chat and watermarked confidential meetings. Pricing for Teams Premium starts at $10 per user per month on top of a Microsoft 365 subscription, with the full Copilot experience requiring a $30 per user per month Copilot add-on. Zoom AI Companion has kept pace with Microsoft through its own substantial AI investments. Zoom's AI features are particularly strong in meeting intelligence, with automatic meeting summaries that capture context, decisions, and action items with high accuracy. The AI Companion can participate in meetings, answering questions in real time without disrupting the conversation flow. Zoom's video AI capabilities include studio effects that use AI to enhance lighting, smooth skin, and maintain eye contact, even when looking at a second monitor. Zoom's intelligent director automatically switches between camera views to show the most active speaker, including when participants are using multiple cameras in a single room. Zoom's translation capabilities support real-time translation in over fifteen languages with natural-sounding voice output. Zoom AI Companion is included at no additional cost for paid Zoom accounts, making it the most accessible enterprise-grade AI video conferencing solution. Both platforms offer extensive integration marketplaces and enterprise-grade security certifications.

## Mid-Market and Innovative Platforms: Google Meet and Cisco Webex

Google Meet and Cisco Webex have emerged as strong competitors in the AI video conferencing space, each leveraging unique strengths. Google Meet, deeply integrated with Google Workspace, has introduced powerful AI features powered by Google's advanced language models. Google's AI enhances video quality through adaptive processing that optimizes bandwidth usage based on connection quality and content type. During meetings, Google's AI provides real-time captioning in multiple languages, noise cancellation that learns from your environment, and automatic framing that keeps participants centered regardless of movement. The Gemini integration in Google Meet is particularly innovative—it can summarize meetings, provide "recap" cards for missed portions, and offer contextual suggestions based on meeting content. Google Meet's AI can also generate custom backgrounds that adapt to room geometry and lighting, creating a more natural appearance than static virtual backgrounds. For organizations already using Google Workspace, Google Meet's AI features provide an excellent integrated experience at no additional cost beyond the workspace subscription, which starts at $12 per user per month for Business plans. Cisco Webex has reinvented itself with AI at the core of its platform, addressing the needs of organizations that require enterprise-grade security, compliance, and reliability. Webex AI features include real-time noise removal powered by deep learning, which Cisco claims eliminates 30 million background noises per day across its platform. The Webex AI Assistant can summarize meetings, capture action items, and answer questions about meeting content. Webex's People Insights feature uses AI to provide context about meeting participants, including their expertise, recent projects, and communication preferences, making introductions and collaboration more effective. For organizations in regulated industries, Webex offers AI features that operate within strict compliance boundaries, including automatic redaction of sensitive information in meeting transcripts and recordings. Webex pricing starts at $25 per user per month for the Business plan, with AI features included. Both platforms offer strong AI capabilities, with Google winning on integration and value for Google Workspace users, and Webex excelling in security and compliance-sensitive environments.

## AI-Native Challengers: Otter AI Meetings and Krisp

While established players integrate AI into existing platforms, a new generation of AI-native meeting tools is redefining what video conferencing can be. Otter AI Meetings has evolved from a transcription tool into a full meeting platform that places AI at the center of the experience. Otter's platform is designed around the premise that AI should manage meeting logistics so participants can focus on discussion. The AI automatically joins scheduled meetings, captures full transcripts with speaker identification, generates comprehensive summaries, and organizes action items. Otter's unique strength is its contextual awareness—the AI understands the content and context of discussions, generating summaries that capture nuance and decisions that other tools miss. The platform's conversation intelligence analytics help teams understand meeting patterns, participation balance, and decision velocity. Otter AI Meetings costs $16.99 per month for the Pro plan or $33.99 per month for Business. Krisp has taken a distinctly different approach, focusing on AI-powered audio enhancement as its core differentiator. Krisp's AI can completely eliminate background noise from any audio source, including barking dogs, construction noise, or crying babies, processing audio bidirectionally to enhance both your outgoing and incoming audio. Beyond noise cancellation, Krisp's 2026 platform includes AI meeting transcription, summarization, and action item extraction that works across any meeting platform. Krisp's unique value is that it operates as an AI layer on top of your existing video conferencing tools, enhancing Zoom, Teams, Google Meet, or any other platform. Krisp pricing starts at $16 per month for the Pro plan, with a generous free tier that includes limited noise cancellation minutes. These AI-native platforms demonstrate that the future of video conferencing belongs to platforms that put intelligence first rather than adding AI as a feature afterthought.

## Emerging and Specialized Solutions: Around and mmhmm

Two emerging platforms deserve attention for their innovative approaches to AI-enhanced video communication. Around has built a video conferencing platform designed specifically for hybrid and asynchronous work. The platform uses AI to create a "voice-first" meeting experience where video is present but not dominant, reducing meeting fatigue. Around's AI manages meeting flow, generating real-time summaries and action items while the meeting progresses. The platform's spatial audio technology uses AI to create a natural soundscape that helps participants distinguish different speakers, particularly important in hybrid scenarios with multiple people in a room. The AI also provides meeting analytics that help teams optimize their meeting culture over time. Mmhmm, originally focused on creative presentation overlays, has evolved into a comprehensive AI video platform that excels at making virtual communication engaging and dynamic. Its AI-powered backdrop technology creates realistic virtual environments that respond to movement and perspective, far surpassing the quality of traditional virtual backgrounds. Mmhmm's AI can transform presentation materials into interactive visual elements that appear alongside the speaker, creating a TV-quality production without dedicated equipment or production staff. While less suited for large enterprise deployments, these platforms offer compelling options for forward-thinking teams that want to reinvent how they communicate virtually.
