I have a confession to make: I've been cheating on ChatGPT with Claude.

For the past few months, whenever I need to actually get work done—writing a tricky email, summarizing a dense research paper, or debugging a script that's making me question my life choices—I've been turning to Anthropic's Claude. It's been my quiet backchannel assistant. Type a prompt, get an answer. Private. Personal. Like having a brilliant intern who never sleeps and doesn't judge me for asking the same question three times.

But here's the thing: that private chat window model is fundamentally broken for how teams actually work. You can't share the conversation. You can't have the AI participate in a group discussion. You end up copy-pasting responses into Slack threads, which is clunky and defeats the purpose.

Anthropic just fixed that. And honestly, it's kind of wild when you think about it.

**What's Actually Happening Here**

According to www.artificialintelligence-news.com, Anthropic launched a beta version of its Claude Tag feature for Enterprise and Team tiers. The headline feature is simple but profound: you can now type `@Claude` in any shared Slack channel, and Claude will respond directly in the thread, visible to everyone.

This isn't just a chatbot that lives in a side panel. It's an AI agent that exists inside your team's conversation space. It can answer questions, summarize threads, generate documents, and even perform multi-step tasks—all within the context of an active group discussion.

I got early access to test this last week, and after spending five days abusing it in a mock team channel, I have strong opinions. Let me walk you through what works, what doesn't, and why this might be the most significant workplace AI integration since ChatGPT plugins.

**The "@Claude" Experience: What It's Like**

First, the setup is refreshingly simple. If your Slack admin has enabled the integration, you just type `@Claude` followed by your request. No new windows. No switching tabs. No awkward "hey everyone, I'm going to check with the AI real quick" moments.

I tested it in a channel I created with a few colleagues who agreed to be guinea pigs. We were working on a project plan for a fictional product launch. Here's what happened:

I typed: `@Claude what are the top three risks for a Q4 launch based on our current timeline?`

Within seconds, Claude responded with a bullet-point list that referenced specific dates and dependencies from our earlier conversation. It had read the thread history. It understood context. It didn't hallucinate some generic startup advice. It actually analyzed the specific constraints we had discussed.

One of my colleagues, who is generally skeptical of AI, typed back: "Wait, did it just... know about the vendor delay we talked about yesterday?"

Yes. Yes, it did.

**Why This Matters (More Than You Think)**

The typical enterprise AI tool works like a vending machine. You walk up to it, insert a prompt, get a result, and walk away. It's a transaction. You don't build a relationship with a vending machine. You don't have a shared context with a vending machine.

But `@Claude` in Slack changes the dynamic entirely. The AI becomes a participant in an ongoing conversation. It can be tagged in a thread, respond, get follow-up questions, refine its answers, and even trigger actions. It's less like a tool and more like a colleague who's always up to speed.

According to www.artificialintelligence-news.com, this integration shifts the model "away from traditional isolated chat boxes." That's an understatement. It fundamentally changes what a workplace AI assistant can be.

**The Good: What I Loved**

**1. Contextual awareness is genuinely impressive.**

I ran a test where I pasted a 20-page project brief into a channel. Then I asked `@Claude` to identify all the ambiguous deadlines. It found three that I missed. One of them was a date that was technically correct in the document but conflicted with a company holiday. It caught that. I didn't.

**2. It handles multi-step tasks gracefully.**

I asked Claude to: "Find the Q3 budget spreadsheet attached to the finance channel, extract the marketing line items, and create a summary table." It did it. It asked for clarification on one ambiguous category. It didn't just guess. It asked. That's huge.

**3. It's fast.**

Responses in Slack are nearly instant. No spinning wheel for 20 seconds. It feels like you're talking to a human who types really fast.

**4. It doesn't break the flow.**

Because it's just another participant in the channel, you don't have to leave Slack. You don't have to open a new tab. You don't have to explain context again. The conversation just continues. This is the killer feature.

**The Bad: What Needs Work**

**1. It's too polite.**

This sounds weird, but Claude in Slack is extremely deferential. It constantly asks permission before doing things. "Would you like me to summarize this thread?" "I can generate a draft if that's helpful." I get that Anthropic is being cautious, but in a fast-moving Slack channel, you want an AI that just does the thing and lets you correct it if needed. The constant asking slows things down.

**2. Visibility is a double-edged sword.**

Everything `@Claude` does is visible to the entire channel. That's great for transparency. But it also means that if Claude makes a mistake—and it will—the whole team sees it. I accidentally asked it to "generate a list of potential vendors" and it pulled in a competitor's name that was clearly wrong. Everyone saw it. Awkward.

**3. It's not great at reading the room.**

If a channel is already in a heated debate about a topic, tagging `@Claude` can feel like inviting a stranger into a family argument. It needs to be more context-aware about tone and timing. Right now, it responds the same way whether the channel is calm or chaotic.

**4. Enterprise tier only.**

This is a beta for Team and Enterprise tiers. If you're on a free or Pro Slack plan, you're out of luck. That's a bummer because smaller teams could benefit the most from this.

**The Unsettling Part: What Does It Mean for Team Dynamics?**

I want to be honest about something: using `@Claude` in a shared channel feels different from using it privately. It changes the social dynamics.

In a private chat, you can ask dumb questions. You can explore wrong ideas. You can say "draft an email that's passive-aggressive but professional" without anyone knowing. In a shared channel, everything is public. Every query is visible. Every response is recorded.

I found myself self-censoring. I didn't want to look like I was relying on AI too much. I didn't want my colleagues to think I couldn't do basic analysis myself. That's a real concern. If everyone starts using `@Claude` in channels, will it create pressure to always have the AI-generated answer? Will people feel like they're falling behind if they don't use it?

Anthropic needs to address this. Maybe a way to have Claude respond in a private thread that only the requester can see. Maybe an option to ask Claude to "think about this and respond when I ask." Something that preserves the privacy of the query while still making the output shareable.

**How It Compares to Other AI Slack Integrations**

This isn't the first AI in Slack. Not by a long shot. There's ChatGPT for Slack (from OpenAI), which has a similar @-mention feature. There's also Zapier's AI integrations, Notion AI's Slack bot, and a bunch of smaller players.

Here's the difference: Claude's integration feels more like a teammate. ChatGPT for Slack is good at answering questions, but it doesn't have the same ability to maintain context over a long conversation. It tends to forget what was said two messages ago. Claude remembers the whole thread. It also feels more cautious, which can be frustrating, but also means it makes fewer confident mistakes.

Zapier's AI is better at actually doing things—creating tickets, updating spreadsheets, sending emails. Claude in Slack is more focused on thinking and analysis. Both are useful. They complement each other.

**The Pricing Question**

Anthropic hasn't announced specific pricing for this Slack integration beyond the standard Claude for Enterprise and Team tiers. Team is $30/user/month. Enterprise is custom. That's not cheap, but for teams that spend hours in Slack every day, the time savings could justify it quickly.

I calculated (roughly) that if `@Claude` saves each team member 30 minutes per week on average, and your team of 10 has an average hourly cost of $75, that's $375 per week saved. Over a year, that's nearly $20,000. The cost of the subscription would be around $3,600 for a team of 10. The math works.

**The Bigger Picture: AI as a Colleague, Not a Tool**

I've been writing about AI for over a decade. I covered the first wave of chatbots, the rise of generative AI, and the current explosion of agents. I've seen a lot of hype. And I've seen a lot of products that promised to change everything but ended up as expensive toys.

This Slack integration feels different. Not because the technology is radically new—it's Claude, which has been around—but because the interface is finally right. Putting the AI inside the conversation, rather than beside it, changes the relationship.

When you have to leave your communication tool to talk to an AI, the AI remains an external resource. Something you go to. Something separate from the team. But when the AI is in the same channel, responding to the same threads, it becomes part of the team. It's a subtle shift, but it matters.

Anthropic seems to understand this. They're not selling Claude as a productivity booster. They're selling it as a team member. And that's a much more interesting proposition.

**What I Want to See Next**

I have a wishlist. It's short:

- **Private mode.** Let me ask Claude something in a channel without everyone seeing the query. The response can be public, but the prompt should be private.
- **Action triggers.** Let Claude actually create Slack messages, schedule meetings, or update project boards. Right now, it mostly talks. I want it to do.
- **Better tone detection.** If the channel is tense, Claude should adjust its response. Maybe be more concise. Maybe wait. Maybe ask if someone wants a response.
- **Custom personas.** Let teams create a "Claude for engineering" that talks like an engineer, or a "Claude for marketing" that has a different voice.

**Final Thoughts**

I started using `@Claude` in my test channel with skepticism. I ended it with grudging respect. It's not perfect. It's too polite, too visible, and too expensive for small teams. But the core idea is right.

We've been trying to put AI into the workplace for years. We've tried chatbots, copilots, plugins, and agents. Most of them feel like add-ons. This one feels native. It feels like Slack was always meant to have an AI participant.

Will it replace human judgment? No. Will it make your team smarter? Probably. Will it make meetings shorter? God, I hope so.

The real test will come when Anthropic opens this up to more users and we see how teams actually use it. Will they treat it like a colleague? A tool? A crutch? I don't know. But I'm excited to find out.

And the next time someone in your Slack channel asks a question that requires a thoughtful, well-researched answer, you might find yourself thinking: "I should just @Claude that."

That's not a bad thing. That's the future.
